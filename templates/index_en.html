The problem

Poverty forces families to choose between food and school

  • 📚
    Lack of school books and uniforms is the most common reason children drop out — even where schooling itself is free.
  • 👧
    For girls, the consequences are especially severe: without schooling, many end up in early marriage as teenagers.
  • 💡
    Education breaks the generational cycle of poverty. It is the single most powerful tool we have.
  • 🤝
    Rise for Africa is already on the ground in Bujumbura with local partners and established programs.

The person behind it

From Burundi to Oslo — and back to make a difference.

Marie-Chantal Nzeyimana grew up in Burundi and knows first-hand what it means to grow up in a country where education is a privilege, not a given. Today she lives in Oslo — but her heart is still in Bujumbura.

"I remember what it was like to grow up without resources, and I remember the feeling of being seen by someone who believed in me. That is exactly what we try to give these children — not just books, but the belief that they have a future." — Marie-Chantal Nzeyimana, Founder of Rise for Africa

Questions & answers

Frequently asked questions

Where does my donation go? +

100% of funds go directly to school supplies (books, uniforms, stationery) and mentorship programs for vulnerable children in the Bujumbura region of Burundi. Rise for Africa works with local schools and partners to ensure transparent use of all donations. GoFundMe provides full visibility into the campaign and regular updates.

Can I donate any amount? +

Yes, absolutely. Every amount helps. kr 500 covers school books for one child. kr 1,000 gives one child a full school year with everything included. kr 5,000 helps 5 children. There is no minimum donation — every contribution counts and makes a direct difference.

Who is Rise for Africa? +

Rise for Africa is a non-profit organisation founded by Marie-Chantal Nzeyimana, based in Oslo, Norway. It offers mentorship programs and school support to vulnerable youth and single mothers in the Bujumbura region of Burundi. In 2025, they helped over 800 children.

How do I know my money is used correctly? +

The campaign runs through GoFundMe, one of the world's most recognized fundraising platforms, with full financial transparency. Marie-Chantal regularly updates the campaign page with photos and reports from the work on the ground. You can follow the progress directly on GoFundMe at any time.

Can I give monthly? +

Yes! GoFundMe supports recurring monthly donations. kr 100 per month = kr 1,200 per year = more than one child funded annually. Ongoing support is especially valuable because it allows Rise for Africa to plan ahead and commit to children's education over time.
